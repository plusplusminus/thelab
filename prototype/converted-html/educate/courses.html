Body Electric Continuing Medical Education

The Spaulding-Labuschagne Neuromodulation Center believes the future of good health lies in the awareness and education of Body Electric. Our course initiative is designed to educate the new advances in the science of Body Electric.

The value of the Center’s education is sharing this knowledge, but if people don’t know how to use the knowledge, it’s value is diminished. The importance of sharing globally is the opportunity for many to participate in these discoveries and bring their own value to the exploration.

Felipe Fregni - Director of the
Spaulding-Labuschagne Neuromodulation Center.

My name is Felipe Fregni and I’m the Director of the Spaulding-Labuschagne Neuromodulation Center.

I’m also Associate Professor at Harvard Medical School, Department of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation and also the Department of Neurology.

I’m very pleased to have the chance to create a curriculum on Body Electric — Think Outside the Brain.

I have been fortunate to explore with Spaulding Hospital and the Harvard Medical School the power of neuro- plasticity and the potential of non-invasive stimulation to both enhance and strengthen neural connections and networks. What is exciting today is recognizing that this plasticity extends throughout the body and that the idea of compartmentalizing of what we call the “mind” as isolated in the brain is no longer valid. Body Electric is understanding that the mind is the neural integration of the entire mass of neural connections through out our body. Body Electric is truly Think Outside the Brain.

Over the last eight years, I have had the pleasure to share my interactive courses in neuroscience and non-invasive neuromodulation in conjunction with the Harvard Medical School to over 500 professionals and students in 40 countries.

In 2016, I will be offering an exciting curriculum in Body Electric through The Labuschagne Foundation.

Body Electric Curriculum


The course will introduce Body Electric as an approach to understanding the bioelectric foundation of the entire body. It will illustrate the integration of neurotechnology and neuroscience as a platform to monitor, diagnosis and treat physical and cognitive disabilities. The course will also discuss the new exploration and clinical studies in neuromodulation as an enhancement in performance and learning.

Leon Morales-Quezada - Associate Research Director of the
Spaulding-Labuschagne Neuromodulation Center.

I’m Leon Morales-Quezada, Associate Research Director of the Spaulding-Labuschagne Neuromodulation Center.

Let me welcome you to Body Electric Webinars - a series designed for anyone interested in learning about the exciting advancements in neuromodulation and understanding of how the brain and body work together as a bioelectric entity.

Body Electric is the outgrowth of both theoretical and clinical research in the growing field of bioelectricity. Our brain, nerves, heart, muscles, gut, senses are electrical—each of us are bodies of natural energy. What does that mean, how does it all work together and how can we harness this to help in healing or individual growth and development, physically and mentally?

Body Electric Webinars are meant to help explore these questions and more for the interested individual, student, or professional. Bioelectricity is not a product of the digital age but has been part of healing history since the Roman physician, Scribonius Larus wrote in 46 AD that patients suffering from gout should stand on live black torpedo fish with their accompanying electric jolts. At the Spaulding-Labuschagne Neuromodulation Center we are daily adding to our knowledge in this field that ranges from physics to neuroscience, neuroplasticity to non-invasive neurostimulation to neurorehabilitation—it’s all Body Electric.

Clinical Research Global Project

The Spaulding-Labuschagne Neuromodulation Center believes that clinicians and health care providers must be trained to critically analyze and implement clinical research data in order to provide the best available treatments to their patients.

We are proud to announce that Professor Felipe Fregni, Director of the Spaulding-Labuschagne Neuromodulation Center, is disseminating this vital information through The Clinical Research Project www.ppcr.org.

Professor Fregni with colleagues throughout the world have developed a highly interactive platform for clinical research training designed for clinicians and junior scientists.

The program also promotes collaborative learning, so participants from the clinical research courses can teach and exchange ideas with their colleagues.  

As of 2015, more than 2000 people have been trained in the Clinical Research Project. Many participants have become very successful clinical researchers and have perpetuated the mission to share this training with colleagues.

The Clinical Research Project Course


The course is a one year duration, and can be followed anywhere in the world through an interactive platform. There are also several live workshops in Boston and Brazil www.ppcr.org. See below video of the interactive final workshop. )

Principles and Practice of Clinical Research covers the basics of clinical research including:

  • How to formulate a research question
  • Selecting a study population with randomization and blinding and statistical methods
  • Data distribution and classification, statistical tests, sample size calculation, survival analysis, missing data and meta-analysis
  • Data collection, monitoring and reporting
  • Training in manuscript writing Study designs: observational studies, non-inferior and adaptive designs and randomized clinical trials
